FAQ

What is SBTI? How is it different from MBTI?
SBTI (Self-Behavioral Type Indicator) is an entertainment-oriented personality test based on the Big Five model with 15-dimension cross-calculation, matching 27 unique personality types. Unlike MBTI's 4-dimension binary approach, SBTI uses a more detailed 3-level assessment (H/M/L) with hidden storylines and special trigger mechanisms. It's not a psychology tool — it's purely for fun.
How long does the test take?
About 30 main questions + 1 hidden trigger question. Typically takes 5-8 minutes. Questions are randomized each time for a slightly different experience.
Are the results accurate?
This test is purely for entertainment — no psychological, medical, or career advice intended. All personality descriptions are written in a humorous tone. If the result fits you, that's a coincidence; if it doesn't, that's also a coincidence.
Is my data uploaded?
No. All calculations run locally in your browser (pure frontend). We do not collect, store, or upload any answer data. Your personality secrets are known only to you.
What is a hidden personality? How to trigger it?
The test contains 1 special trigger question. If your answer meets certain conditions, the system bypasses normal matching and assigns you a hidden personality directly. The exact trigger conditions are secret — just follow your instincts.
Does SBTI support MBTI cross-analysis?
Yes! After completing the test, you can select your MBTI type (e.g., INFP, INTJ) on the results page. The system generates an exclusive MBTI × SBTI cross-analysis. 16 MBTI types × 27 SBTI types = 432 unique combinations.
Can I share my results?
Yes. The results page provides a sharing feature. You can share your personality type result to social media directly from the results page.
Why do I get different results each time?
Questions are randomized each time, and different pacing may affect your choices. Also, if your dimension scores are near boundary values, small changes can match you to different personality types. That's part of what makes the test fun.
